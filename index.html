Intro

I'm a professional molocular biologist + plant ecologist, and an amateur donut enthusiast. I grew up near Kansas City, Missouri, playing in creeks and biking around town. When I applied to college, I took the opportunity to attend Whittier College under an academic scholarship and live in the ecologically-diverse LA area. I focused on Enviornmental Science and Biology...and spending time poking around the local tide pools.

After college, I decided to move somewhere in the midwest with a beach: so off to Chicago I went. (OK, the Lake Superior beach isn't quite the same as the Pacific Ocean, but it still counts as a beach!) Ten years later, I still live in north Chicago with my husband and pony-sized dog. While I've been immersed in my graduate studies at the University of Chicago the past several years, I'm excited to graduate in August 2023 and transition into a career in biotechnoloogy.

Please check out my work, project portfolio , and CV

Work

I joined the Bergelson lab in 2016 as a graduate student at University of Chicago, and moved with the lab to NYU in 2021. During the move, I ported and quality checked approximately 105 TB of historical data to the new institution. I also served as the lab manager, helping manage USDA permits, lab safety training, chemical stock management, and general purchasing.

During grad school, I was interested in delineating the microbe-microbe interactions versus the plant host genotype effects on the emergent microbial community. My current research focuses on the heritability of the A. thaliana leaf microbiome, incorporating microbial interactions and network analysis to better resolve host genotype effects.

Given the complexity of these natural microbial communities, I was also interested in reducing technical noise by improving microbial taxonomic identification in amplicon sequencing. I've been working on building a new gyrB marker gene database using publicly available data in combination with ~4,000 isolates I collected from wild A. thaliana plants from Sweden.

I look forward to transitioning to the biotech industry, where I hope to use my technical computational experience in combination with my hands-on lab experience to contribute to the success of the teams I'm fortunate to join.

Projects

I love automating processes and visualizing data. Here are select projects I've developed in my research.


Web App for Inventory Tracking of Bacteria Isolates

As a part of my graduate studies, I developed protocols to collect and sequence nearly 5,000 bacterial isolates from Arabidopsis thaliana rosette leaves, with essential assistance from Feng Huang (post-doc) and Riley Leff (undergraduate). To make this resource more accessible to future researchers, I developed an interactive web application to allow users to search for isolates based on taxonomy, location, or even amplicon sequence identity using BLAST. Check it out here




High throughput image processing

For the 2023 PNAS manuscript , I wrote a python script to analyze plant area in a gnotobiotic plate well. Images were taken at the same height for each plant on a light box. The script looks for circles, crops into an square with the circle transcribed, and converts green pixels to black (thresholds are defined in the code). Then, the area of the circle that is black is counted. Pixels are converted to mm by using the known well size as the conversion factor, and eported to a csv summary. Binary images are saved to an output dirctory for manual review.
